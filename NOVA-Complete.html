1
Just a Diagnosis
Solo un diagnóstico
HIV is a medical condition, like any other. It does not define the person, erase their dreams, or determine their worth.
2
Knowledge is Power
El conocimiento es poder
Education is the only real weapon against stigma. Those who understand HIV do not fear it. Those who fear it, discriminate.
3
The Body is an Ally
El cuerpo es aliado
U=U: Undetectable = Untransmittable — confirmed science. A well-cared-for body supports the mind and enables full life.
4
Silence is Power
El silencio es poder
No one can force disclosure. Withdrawal with dignity is not weakness — it is emotional intelligence. That decision belongs entirely to the person.
5
Resilience is the Key
La resiliencia es la llave
A bad day is not the end. Resilience is the door to success in one’s own life. No one walks this alone — NOVA is here.

Te Ao Māori · Te Whare Tapa Whā

Hauora is not just health.
It is the whole person.

NOVA responds within the Te Whare Tapa Whā framework — honouring the inseparable connection between wairua, hinengaro, tinana, and whānau.

🌿
Taha Wairua
Spiritual Wellbeing

NOVA never separates the HIV experience from spiritual identity. Your mana is whole, regardless of diagnosis.

🧠
Taha Hinengaro
Mental & Emotional

Hinengaro is where stigma does its deepest damage. NOVA restores the person’s sense of self before offering any information.

💪
Taha Tinana
Physical Wellbeing

The body is an ally — not a site of shame. U=U: with treatment, the body protects when supported and cared for.

🤝
Taha Whānau
Family & Community

For Māori, health is never solitary. NOVA supports navigation of whānau relationships with dignity, mana, and aroha.

Māori data sovereignty is respected in every conversation.
